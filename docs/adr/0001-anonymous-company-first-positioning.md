# Anonymous company-first positioning on the public site

Seersware's website presents the firm as a company with no named individuals — no team page, no founder photo, no personal bio. This is deliberate: the site must signal a genuine consulting firm rather than a personal services wrapper, to avoid CRA classification as a Personal Services Business (PSB). Naming the principal consultant prominently would reinforce the single-person narrative that PSB classification relies on. A future reader adding a team page or "meet our founder" section should understand this is not an oversight — it is a regulatory positioning choice.

## Considered Options

- **Named principal consultant (Option B)**: Would feel more credible and personal, but risks reinforcing the PSB argument that the firm exists solely to deliver one person's labour.
- **Founder featured prominently (Option C)**: Standard for personal brands; ruled out entirely for the same reason.

## Consequences

All copy must speak in the company's voice. Any case studies, results, or testimonials must reference Seersware as the delivering entity, not an individual.
